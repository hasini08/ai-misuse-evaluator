How to Score

For each AI use case, give two scores using the scales below. Please read the definitions carefully before you start — the anchors matter.

📊 Scale A — Ethical Risk (1 to 5)

ScoreWhat it meansExample
1No meaningful risk to individuals or societyA tool that predicts weather from satellite data
2Minor privacy or fairness implications, but well-controlled by designA chatbot that stores conversation history but you can delete it
3Significant risk that needs active management; could cause harm if misusedA facial recognition system used in airports
4High risk of harm to specific groups of people; hard to fully mitigateA tool that automatically screens job applicants using past hiring data
5Severe or systematic harm; or the tool is designed to cause harm as its main functionA bot that generates fake political news at scale

🔍 Scale B — Transparency / Explainability (1 to 5)

This asks: if this AI tool made a decision that affected you, could you understand why?

ScoreWhat it meansExample
1Completely opaque — no explanation given, impossible to auditA black-box model where you just get a "yes/no" with no reason
2Very limited — maybe a confidence percentage but no real explanation"Your application was rejected" — no further detail
3Partial — some explanation available (e.g. highlights key factors) but not full reasoning"We rejected you mainly due to credit score" — but doesn't say what score
4Good — clear, human-readable explanations available for most decisionsA medical tool that lists which symptoms drove its diagnosis
5Fully transparent — the reasoning can be completely audited and verifiedA rule-based system or physics model where every step is checkable
